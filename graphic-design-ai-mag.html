ai magazine

A group project done in collaboration with: Julia Can, Tasa Kriznar and Paula Lucane.

Download the interactive pdf here: AI magazine

Tools used: Adobe InDesign, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Midjourney

This 24-page magazine project follows Karl Aspelund's seven stages of design. Inspiration for this project came from a Twitter post by AI researcher Eliezer Yudkowsky, who engaged ChatGPT in a creative experiment. This sparked the theme of artificial intelligence and its dual nature.

The idea of contrasting good and bad through color was explored using black and white. To enhance the visual representation of duality, anaglyph design was employed. A symbolic connection was made with human blood vessels, representing interconnectedness with the machine.

The printed magazine is designed to be readable from both ends, with one side showcasing the positive aspects of AI and the other side focusing on the negative. This unique format emphasizes the balanced perspective, allowing readers to explore both the benefits and potential drawbacks of AI without pushing them toward either extreme.
